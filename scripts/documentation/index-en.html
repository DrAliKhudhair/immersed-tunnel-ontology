Immersed Tunnel Engineering Ontology - GBT 51318-2019 Compliant: Description back to ToC

Comprehensive immersed tunnel engineering ontology aligned with Chinese National Standard GBT 51318-2019 "Immersed Tunnel Design Standard". This ontology provides standardized semantic infrastructure for tunnel engineering design, construction, and operation processes. STANDARDS COMPLIANCE: - GBT 51318-2019: Chinese National Standard for Immersed Tunnel Design (Primary Reference) - IFC4.3: Building SMART International Foundation Classes for Infrastructure - ISO 19650: Organization and digitization of information about buildings and civil engineering works - JTG 2120-2020: Code for Design of Highway Tunnels (Supporting Reference) CORE TERMINOLOGY DEFINITIONS (GBT 51318-2019 Section 2.1): - Immersed tunnel: A tunnel constructed underwater by floating, immersing, and connecting prefabricated structural units within aquatic environments - Element: The fundamental structural unit of an immersed tunnel that is prefabricated and designed for floating, immersion, and underwater connection processes - Floating transportation: The controlled floating and towing of prefabricated tunnel elements on water surface to designated installation positions - Immersion: The precise sinking process of tunnel elements into predetermined underwater positions - Connection: The alignment and joining process between tunnel elements or between elements and connection segments DESIGN PRINCIPLES (GBT 51318-2019 Section 1): - Structural safety and reliability ensuring integrity under all loading conditions - Technical advancement incorporating engineering best practices and innovations - Economic optimization balancing cost-effectiveness with performance requirements - Durability assurance for long-term operational viability SCOPE OF APPLICATION: - Survey and design of immersed tunnel structures utilizing reinforced concrete elements - Implementation of immersed tunnel construction methodologies - Compliance with national standards and regulatory requirements - Support for digital twin and BIM workflows MODULAR ARCHITECTURE: - 4 Foundation Modules: Core semantic infrastructure and taxonomic organization - 15 Domain Modules: Specialized engineering domains with comprehensive coverage - Standards-compliant terminology and definitions throughout all modules - IFC4.3 integration readiness for buildingSMART standardization
